MY STORY

My name is Brigi Horváth and I am a Hungarian from Denmark.

I am very-very happy to be a part of this scholarship. Actually I hope that it could help me to switch to an IT career.

Originally I was an economist at a telecommunication company. Huge company, lot of tasks and I was responsible that our marketing campaigns are implemented well in our IT systems...like some kind of translator between the marketing and the IT team. I felt that I am hugely interested in IT, because I wanted to show to all the people in the world that it is possible to build better (I mean also functionally and UX-wise) systems and websites, that existed at that time at that company. I also started a second degree on a University in Computer Sciences faculty, but after two years I got a promotion and a lot of tasks with a lot of overtime. I couldn't go on with my studies. (Actually I tried, but I was so eexhausted that I decided to protect my health and not do these two things together.)

So I was working as an economist and then in 2013 my son was born. I was very happy to be with him at home, but I felt that I lose my brain capacity, so I knew that I should learn something to maintain the numbers of my grey cells. That was when I started to learn web-development, on my own, at home. I really enjoyed it and I made some stuff, but when I went back to work after my maternity leave I have to stop again.

In 2017, my husband got an opportunity to work in Denmark, so we - together with our son, of course - moved to Denmark. That was also the year when I applied to the Google and Udacity joint Front-End Developer Nanodegree scholarship and I got in!

I am also learning Danish and I hope that I will have an opportunity, to work in the IT sector, after all.
